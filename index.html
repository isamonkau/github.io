1. AI News in the World: Trends & Developments

Multimodal Image Generation: A New Level of Creativity

In recent weeks, both Google and OpenAI have achieved a breakthrough with multimodal image generation. This means that AI can now directly create images with the same precision as text, token by token. Where previously generated text on images often changed into illegible scribbles, AI can now create professional-looking visual content with readable texts.

This technology opens up a world of new possibilities for creative professionals. The combination of precision and dialogue makes this technology particularly powerful, as you can directly consult with the AI about your images and refine them until they're perfect.

This technology is now accessible via a free ChatGPT account, although there are limitations on the number of images you can create per day. With a paid account, you can generate images faster and without limits.

Vibe Coding: Programming Without Code

A second fascinating development is "vibe coding," a revolutionary way to create digital products without programming knowledge. The term describes a process where you simply explain in plain language what you want to build, including the desired "vibe" or style, after which the AI generates working code.

In essence, this means: you tell AI what you want to build in natural language, and the AI converts this into working code. You don't need to program; you describe your idea, iterate a bit, and you have a working app or website. This not only speeds up the innovation process but also opens new doors for creativity because technical complexity is no longer a limitation.

This website, for example, is made completely through vibe coding in Claude AI.

2. Trends in the Advertisement Industry: AI's Growing Influence

Revolutionizing Media Workflows with AI

The media landscape is undergoing a profound transformation as artificial intelligence reshapes traditional workflows. According to TV Technology, forward-thinking media organizations are discovering that AI implementation goes far beyond simple automation—it's about fundamentally reimagining how content is created, processed, and delivered.

The most successful implementations are occurring in stages: starting with clearly defined problems, implementing focused AI solutions, and then gradually expanding capabilities. Media companies report significant benefits including streamlined content processing, reduced operational costs, and the ability to repurpose valuable creative talent toward higher-value tasks.

What's particularly exciting is how AI is enhancing creative possibilities rather than limiting them. Content creators now have tools that can handle routine tasks like metadata tagging, content categorization, and technical quality checks automatically, allowing more time for strategic and creative thinking.

Agentic AI: The Next Evolution in Advertising Technology

The IAB identifies this development as potentially transformative for the advertising industry. These systems can now navigate multi-step processes autonomously, from audience research and creative development to media placement optimization and performance analysis.

Agentic AI represents a significant leap forward from traditional AI applications. While conventional AI systems respond to specific prompts, agentic AI operates with greater autonomy—independently performing complex sequences of tasks with minimal human intervention.

Spotify's AI-Powered Advertising Revolution

Spotify recently showcased their latest advances in AI-driven advertising at their Spotify Advance event. Their platform has evolved from a simple music streaming service into a sophisticated advertising ecosystem powered by intelligent algorithms. The three key takeaways for advertisers highlight how AI is creating unprecedented opportunities:

  1. Contextual Intelligence: Spotify's AI now analyzes not just listener demographics but contextual factors including listening environment, time of day, device type, and content sequencing patterns. This enables advertisers to reach audiences in moments when they're most receptive to specific messaging.
  2. Personalization at Scale: Their new technology can generate thousands of creative variations automatically, tailoring message components, tone, and even music beds to individual listener preferences while maintaining brand consistency.
  3. Predictive Engagement Metrics: Perhaps most impressively, their platform now offers predictive analytics that forecast how different audience segments will respond to various creative approaches before campaigns launch.

As Spotify noted in their April 2nd announcement, "We're moving beyond traditional targeting to create truly intelligent audio experiences where advertisements feel as personalized and relevant as the content itself."

Data Unification: WPP's Strategic Investment in AI-Driven Solutions

WPP's recent acquisition of InfoSum signals a major strategic shift in how advertising conglomerates are approaching datamanagement and privacy challenges. This $150 million investment underscores the critical importance of sophisticated data infrastructure in the evolving advertising landscape.

InfoSum's technology enables advertisers to connect diverse data sources without actually moving or sharing the underlying information—a capability that grows increasingly valuable as privacy regulations tighten and third-party cookies phase out. The platform uses advanced AI to derive insights while maintaining strict data separation protocols.

Mark Read, CEO of WPP, described the acquisition as "building the industry's most comprehensive AI-driven data capability" that will enable clients to "deliver more personalized experiences with greater precision while respecting consumer privacy."

This development illustrates how AI is not just changing campaign execution but fundamentally transforming the infrastructure that supports modern advertising operations.

Looking Forward: Strategic Considerations for Media Leaders

As these technologies continue reshaping the advertising landscape, media leadership would be wise to consider:

  • Which specific workflow challenges in your organization could benefit most from targeted AI solutions?
  • How might agentic AI enhance your team's capabilities without replacing their essential creative judgment?
  • What data unification strategies will best position your organization for success in an increasingly privacy-focused ecosystem?

The organizations that thrive will be those that view AI not as a replacement for human creativity but as a powerful tool that amplifies it—allowing teams to focus on strategic thinking while automation handles the routine.

3. People & Leadership

AI Makes Leaders More Human

As AI takes over more tasks, an interesting paradox emerges: this technology can actually encourage more human leadership. Recent research by leadership experts Rasmus Hougaard and Jacqueline Carter, described in their book "More Human," shows that AI offers three important benefits for leaders.

First, leaders achieve significant time savings on administrative tasks. A CHRO at Accenture discovered that AI reduced the time spent gathering information for performance reviews from 45 minutes to just 5 minutes. This creates space for what truly matters: listening to employees and paying attention to their development.

Second, AI offers possibilities for customization in leadership style by helping to analyze individual needs and preferences of team members. And finally, AI can support strengthening human qualities such as empathy and awareness. At Mastercard, chief people officer Michael Fraccaro is experimenting with an AI coach, with which he practices challenging conversations in the workforce and receives recommendations.

The challenge for modern leaders is to use AI not to replace human contact, but to deepen it.

"We encourage you to think about how you interact with your people. What experience can you give them, so that the meeting, email, or text message really becomes worthwhile and cannot be replicated by a machine." - Hougaard and Carter

AI as your Teammate

A large-scale experiment at Procter & Gamble, conducted by Harvard and Wharton Business School with 776 experienced professionals, shows fascinating results about AI as a teammate in professional environments.

  • Better performance: one person with AI can achieve comparable results to a two-person team, while a team using AI achieves the very best results.
  • Breaking down silos: AI helps specialists embrace each other's fields. In the experiment, the boundaries between R&D professionals and commercial experts completely blurred: R&D people came up with strongly commercial ideas, and commercial people devised technical solutions.
  • Positive work experience: Technological changes are often associated with more stress and less job satisfaction. In this experiment, AI users actually experienced more positive emotions (such as enthusiasm and energy) and suffered less from negative feelings like anxiety and frustration.

These insights show that AI is more than a tool that automates existing tasks. It's a teammate that structurally changes our way of working and the structure of teams.

4. Personal Tips & Experiments

Writing Effective AI Prompts

A good prompt is the key to effective use of generative AI. The following structure helps you get clear and focused results:

1

Assign a specific expertise or role to the AI that fits your question

2

Clearly define who the answer is intended for and what you want to achieve with it

3

Specify the desired tone, length, and format of the answer

Example prompt:

"You are a prominent media innovator with deep knowledge of the changing media landscape in Europe. I would like you to answer my question in a way that is inspiring for the strategic management of our media company. What will the revenue model look like in 5 years in a world dominated by streaming platforms and personalized content? Give your answer concisely in one paragraph."

(A)Inception Prompting tip: Have AI write or improve your prompts! A useful approach is, for example: "Improve this prompt: [your prompt idea]." This helps create a structured prompt that is easily readable for AI.
